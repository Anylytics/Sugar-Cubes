Welcome!

This website is an exploratory project into effective visual representation of various foods and the nutrients they contain. The original data source comes from the USDA's National Nutrient Database for Standard Reference. These apps make use of a SQLite database provided in a GitHub repo. Sugar Explorer

This little web app was inspired by a Reddit post about visualizing the amount of sugar in a Red Bull using sugar cubes. The app allows you to search for food items, and it will visualize the amount of sugar in it using sugar cubes on a scale. Click below to get started!

Food Index Explorer

The USDA Nutrient Database includes a wealth of nutritional data from the basics like sugar, fat and protein content to more obscure measures such as the exact amount of each amino acid it contains. There are 146 nutrients defined in total across 8194 food items. The Food Index explorer looks to boil the nutrients in a food down to a single number that can provide a relative rating of that food's 'healthiness' factor using an algorithm from a paper published by the Center for Public Health Nutrition, School of Public Health, University of Washington
